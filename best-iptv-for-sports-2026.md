### 2. Server Reliability During Peak Events

The real test of an IPTV service is a Champions League final or a World Cup match — when millions are watching simultaneously. Look for:

- **Anti-freeze technology** — providers that use server redundancy with automatic failover
- **Uptime guarantees** — 99.9% or higher
- **No history of outages** during major events

### 3. Stream Quality

Sports demands high bitrate streams to handle fast motion without artifacts. Requirements:

- **4K Ultra HD** for flagship events on compatible channels
- **Full HD (1080p)** as the minimum for all sports content
- **Low latency** — ideally under 10 seconds behind the live broadcast

### 4. EPG and Catch-Up

A proper electronic program guide lets you see what's on across all sports channels at a glance. Catch-up TV (replaying events from the past 24–72 hours) is essential for fans in different time zones.

### 5. Multi-Device and Simultaneous Connections

You should be able to watch the football on the TV while someone else watches tennis on a tablet. Look for plans that include at least 2 simultaneous connections.

### 6. Price and Trial

A good provider lets you test the service before committing. Look for:

- Free trial period (24 hours minimum)
- Money-back guarantee (7 days is standard)
- Transparent pricing with no hidden fees
